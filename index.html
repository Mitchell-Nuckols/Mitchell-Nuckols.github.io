Oh hey look, a blog

Alright. So this thing is just a blog that I will be (hopefully) keeping up to date on just random things I do.

So, about me. My name is Mitchell Nuckols and as of time that I am writing this first page, I am 15 years old. I want to go to college for computer engineering and then take that degree and do something with it. This blog is mainly gonna be just an account of what I do involving various things I am interested in. This includes, but is not limited to, computer engineering, computer proggramming, and studying Japanese. So, though this is more of a personal blog, feel free to read it if you want to.


My interest in computer engineering started a couple years ago. I would say maybe around the time I was 12 or 13. I had been a big Minecraft -err- fan back then. What initally drew me into the game when I was 11 was redstone. I saw people do things on YouTube with them and with me already being interested in basic electronics, I was pretty excited to mess around with the stuff. I eventually found out about computational redstone, which deals with different things from building simple logic gates to entire cpus. Though I never actually built a cpu in Minecraft (yet), I still was able to build alus and stuff and learned quite a bit about low level computing concepts from people in that community. I took this idea of building low level computer technology and decided I wanted to do it as my job some day.

In order to prepare for this, I need to start self studying. I plan to read The Elements of Computing Systems by Noam Nisan and Shimon Schocken as a starter and then move on from there. Basic computer science (pretty much computer programming) is something I am still interested in, but not something I would want to do as a job. I find it way cooler building the things that computer scientists program on. I like to think of computer engineering as a mix between computer science and electrical engineering (which it literally is, my definition is no different from anyone elses').

Also, I would like to apologize in advance for my terrible html/css skills. Like, 4th grader who plays around with html on his grandparents' computer bad. It works I guess. I don't need anything too complicated, so I just use Bootstrap for fancy fonts and the grid system and then do really stupid stuff with divs and things for any extra formatting. I just don't want to bother setting up any blogging software so long as putting a new 'div class="row"' and then doing my stuff in paragraph tags works.

Also, it seems super weird to me when I type something out in a blog post. What I do is use words like "our" or "you" which is kinda weird. I guess I could consider it as the readers and I as a collective, but I mostly am talking to myself. This blog is like a notebook for me. I rarely talk to or refer to anyone else. When I say something like "This is our goal", this is me pretty much telling myself about my goal because, let's face it, I am shit at explanations of things, so I don't expect anyone to learn from what I say. (Notice me using "let's" when I'm the only one reading this) If you do though, that's cool.
