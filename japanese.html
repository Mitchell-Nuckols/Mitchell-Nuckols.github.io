8/25 - "Please don't resell tickets at a high price"

Translated from here on 9/8/17

There are people who buy tickets from their original price and then sell them for a very high price on the Internet.

On the 23rd, 4 organizations in the music profession and more than 100 singers said, “Please don’t sell tickets for a high price in order to make a profit.” They are saying that fans cannot enjoy concerts because tickets are being sold at a very high price.

On the Internet, there are even tickets that are being sold for more than 10 times the price. Regulations of Japanese prefectures ban the selling of tickets at a high price near the concert venues. Music organizations and singers are saying that they want the selling of tickets at a high price banned on the Internet, too.



9/8 - Paralympics Begin in Rio de Janeiro

Translated from here on 9/8/17

Rio de Janeiro, Brazil – the Paralympics opening ceremony was held on the 7th.

For the beginning of the ceremony, people riding on wheel chairs descended a slope 17m tall at amazing speed, and then did a big jump. After that, the people in wheelchairs and people with no disabilities danced together.

And then, athletes from 159 countries and regions came into the hall, standing side by side. Japanese athletes were the 82nd to enter. Wheelchair tennis player, Kamijiyui, was the one holding the Japanese flag.

In conclusion, Brazilian players lit the Olympic torch relay inside the ceremony hall. There are 4,400 players participating in the Paralympics and will perform in 22 games over 18 days.



9/7 - Asakusa Shopping District: Please don't eat while walking

Translated from here on 9/7/17

The Tokyo shopping district is a tourist attraction popular among foreigners as well. In the Asakusa shopping district, there are people who eat soft-serve ice cream, dango, etc. while walking around and then also touch shops’ items with grimy hands.

Because of this, the shopping district wrote “Please do not eat while walking” in Japanese, English, and Chinese on a picture of an ogre about 1 m tall and put it in the street. And then, passed out papers with the same thing written on them to people who came to Asakusa. Places to sit and eat food have also been created.

People of the shopping district are saying, “We are worried because we cannot sell goods that have become dirty. Regarding food, please be sure to eat in front of the shops that sell it.”



9/7 - Line Begins to Sell Smartphones That Can Be Used Inexpensively

Translated from here on 9/6/17 (Japan is ahead 17 hours, so that is why the date is behind)

Line is a service that allows the sending of messages with a smartphone, etc. From the 5th of this month, Line has begun to sell smartphones that can be used inexpensively.

The cheapest usage charge for 1 month is 500￥. Regular phones cannot do that, but people who are using the line service can use their telephones and send messages. Even when using this service a lot, the service fee is similar.

In addition, [customers] can transmit up to 1Gb. For emails, 1Gb can be sending and receiving about 2000 of them. That can be watching about 4 hours of Internet videos. A lot of companies are selling smartphones that can be used inexpensively, and competition looks like it is becoming more furious because of this.
